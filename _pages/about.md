Hello! I'm a fourth-year CS PhD student at [EPFL](https://www.epfl.ch/en/), where I am fortunate to be advised by [Dr. Babak Falsafi](https://parsa.epfl.ch/~falsafi/) in the [PARSA Lab](https://parsa.epfl.ch).

I've always been fascinated by computers; not only how we use them, but how we build them. It never ceases to amaze me how much computing has advanced in just over a century, and I hope to contribute, in however small a way, to its continued progress.

My current research focuses on designing area- and power-efficient CPUs for server workloads. As we navigate the post-Moore era, improving performance increasingly depends on making the most of limited silicon area and power budgets rather than relying on process scaling alone.

Before diving into computer architecture, I worked on ML model compression to make large language models more efficient, as well as verification techniques for analog circuits. These experiences sparked my appreciation for systems across the computing stack and continue to shape the way I approach research today.
